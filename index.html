Royal Jewelery Museum

The Royal Jewelry Museum is a museum that displays the jewelry of the royal families that ruled Egypt, and is located in the city of Alexandria. The palace was built in 1919 in the Zizinia area. It is an architectural masterpiece. It has an area of ​​4185. It was developed for Princess Fatima al-Zahra, one of the princesses of the royal family. It was transformed into the Royal Library in the year 1986, and the museum includes a collection of gold jewelry and antiques of the upper royal family dating back to the year 1805, rare artifacts from Muhammad Ali Pasha to Farouk I. The jewelry was encountered by July 1952 and was registered as a museum in 1999, the museum is currently 11 thousand 500 pieces. Read more
